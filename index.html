Tupac Shakur was born in East Harlem, New York City, on June 16, 1971. He spent his early years in this vibrant neighborhood before moving to Baltimore, Maryland, and ultimately rising to fame in the West Coast hip-hop scene.
Tupac Shakur's stepfather was Mutulu Shakur, a prominent Black activist and member of the Black Panther Party. Mutulu was a strong influence on Tupac's early life and activism, as he was actively involved in civil rights and social justice movements.
One of Tupac Shakur's most famous songs is "California Love," released in 1995. This iconic track, featuring Dr. Dre and Roger Troutman, is renowned for its catchy melody, West Coast hip-hop vibe, and memorable lyrics, making it a timeless anthem in the world of rap music.
